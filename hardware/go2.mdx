## What is the Go2?

The **Unitree Go2** is a quadruped ("robot dog") from [Unitree Robotics](https://www.unitree.com/go2/) with 12 actuated joints (three per leg), weighing roughly 15 kg. It carries Unitree's in-house 4D LiDAR for 360° sensing, onboard cameras, and an onboard computer, and reaches speeds of up to ~5 m/s. As a low-cost autonomous mobility platform with an SDK on its higher-end models, it has become one of the most widely adopted legged robots for inspection, education, research, and commercial deployments.

On Cyberwave, the Go2 streams pose, joint states, video, and LiDAR into its digital twin in real time, and the dashboard can drive it back through the edge — including **autonomous waypoint navigation** out of the box. Add it from the catalog, pair the hardware, and control it from the dashboard or the [Python SDK](/tools/python-sdk). See the [robotic dogs overview](/get-started/robotic-dogs) for waypoint missions and inspection workflows.
