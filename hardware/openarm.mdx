## What is the OpenArm?

The **OpenArm** is a fully open-source 7-DOF robotic arm developed by [Enactic](https://github.com/enactic/openarm) for physical-AI research in contact-rich environments. It uses quasi-direct-drive, backdrivable actuators (over a CAN-FD bus) for torque control and safe human-robot interaction, supports a bimanual leader–follower configuration for teleoperation and imitation-learning data collection, and integrates with the Hugging Face LeRobot library. Its hardware is released under CERN-OHL-S and its software under Apache-2.0, making it a flexible platform for collecting demonstration data and training learned policies.

On Cyberwave, the OpenArm uses the same digital-twin workflow as every other arm: add it from the catalog, pair the hardware, then teleoperate it, record datasets, train policies, and deploy them back — all from the dashboard or the [Python SDK](/tools/python-sdk). See the [robotic arms overview](/get-started/robotic-arms) for the full lifecycle from teleoperation to trained VLA policies.
