## Goals

This guide walks you through connecting a physical microphone (external USB mic or a laptop's built-in microphone) to its digital twin on Cyberwave. By the end, you will have:

- Created a Cyberwave environment with a microphone digital twin
- Installed the Cyberwave edge stack on your device
- Paired the physical microphone with the digital twin and streaming audio in real time

Once connected, audio can feed into [ML Models](/use-cyberwave/ml-models) for voice command recognition, anomaly detection, or transcription, and trigger [Workflows](/use-cyberwave/workflows) on spoken input.

---

## Prerequisites

- A USB microphone or a laptop with a built-in microphone
- The microphone should be connected and recognised by your operating system
- Verify your microphone is detected:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Linux">
    ```bash
    arecord -l
    ```
    You should see at least one capture device listed (e.g., `card 1: USB [USB Audio Device]`).
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="macOS">
    ```bash
    ffmpeg -f avfoundation -list_devices true -i "" 2>&1 | grep -A 20 "AVFoundation audio"
    ```
    You should see your microphone listed (e.g., "MacBook Pro Microphone"). Requires `ffmpeg` (`brew install ffmpeg`).
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Phase 1: Configure Cyberwave Environment

Set up your environment and add a microphone digital twin on Cyberwave.

### Step 1: Create an Environment

An [environment](/get-started/key-concepts#environment) is a 3D virtual space that mirrors your real-world setup.

1. Go to the Cyberwave dashboard and click **New Environment**.
2. Give your environment a **name** and **description**.

<Info>
For more details, see [Step 1 of the Quick Start guide](/get-started/quickstart#step-1-create-an-environment).
</Info>

### Step 2: Add the Microphone Digital Twin

1. Inside your environment, click **Add from Catalog** in the left panel.
2. Search for **Microphone** and add it to the environment.
3. Position the twin alongside the hardware or robot it will be paired with.

---

## Phase 2: Install the Cyberwave Edge Stack

Install the Cyberwave CLI and Edge Core on the device where the microphone is plugged in (laptop, edge device, or the robot's host machine).

```bash
curl -fsSL https://cyberwave.com/install.sh | bash
sudo cyberwave edge install
```

Follow the prompts to log in, select your environment, and pair the microphone twin. The appropriate driver will be installed automatically.

<Info>
See [Quick Start > Part 2](/get-started/quickstart#part-2-connect-real-hardware) for the full edge install flow.
</Info>

---

## Phase 3: Verify Live Audio

Open your environment in the Cyberwave dashboard and switch to **Live Mode**. The microphone twin should show an active audio stream.

<Check>
Your microphone is paired with its digital twin. Audio is streaming from the edge device to Cyberwave in real time.
</Check>
