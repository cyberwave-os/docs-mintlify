## What is the SO-101?

The **SO-101** is a low-cost, fully open-source 6-DOF robotic arm designed by [The Robot Studio](https://github.com/TheRobotStudio/SO-ARM100) in collaboration with Hugging Face for the [LeRobot](https://huggingface.co/docs/lerobot/so101) project. It ships as a leader–follower pair driven by Feetech STS3215 bus servos: you hand-guide the leader arm to teleoperate the follower, and the recorded trajectories become training data for imitation learning. It is the successor to the SO-ARM100, released under the Apache-2.0 license, which has made it a popular platform for collecting datasets and training Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models like SmolVLA.

On Cyberwave, the SO-101 ships with a pre-built driver that installs automatically when you pair it with a digital twin. Once paired, you can teleoperate it, record LeRobot-format datasets, train policies, and deploy them back to hardware — all from the dashboard or the [Python SDK](/tools/python-sdk). See the [robotic arms overview](/get-started/robotic-arms) for how arms work end-to-end on Cyberwave.
