## What is a Camera Twin?

A **camera twin** is a digital representation of a physical camera within your Cyberwave environment. It streams live video feed from your hardware camera into the platform, enabling real-time monitoring, data recording, and AI-powered vision workflows.

Cyberwave supports a wide range of cameras:

- **USB webcams** (Logitech, generic UVC-compatible cameras)
- **Laptop built-in webcams**
- **RTSP/IP cameras**
- **NVR-backed camera channels** exposed as RTSP streams

Once connected, your camera feed is available across the platform, in the environment viewer, for dataset recording, and as input to ML models.

## IP Cameras and NVRs

Cyberwave camera twins can also represent IP cameras and NVR-backed channels, not just local USB devices.

- Use **one camera twin per RTSP stream**
- For NVR setups, map each channel to its own twin
- Configure the stream source with the camera driver's `video_device` runtime setting

Typical examples:

```text
rtsp://user:password@camera.local:554/stream1
rtsp://user:password@nvr.local:554/unicast/c1/s1/live
```

In production, prefer running these streams through **Cyberwave Edge Core** so the driver receives the full twin context and lifecycle management automatically.
